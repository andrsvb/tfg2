  1. Question

    What is the derivative of f(x)=x9e2.7xf(x) = x^{9} e^{2.7 x}, evaluated at x=0.55x = 0.55?


    Solution

    Using the product rule for f(x)=g(x)⋅h(x)f(x) = g(x) \cdot h(x), where g(x):=x9g(x) := x^{9} and h(x):=e2.7xh(x) := e^{2.7 x}, we obtain f′(x)=[g(x)⋅h(x)]′=g′(x)⋅h(x)+g(x)⋅h′(x)=9x9−1⋅e2.7x+x9⋅e2.7x⋅2.7=e2.7x⋅(9x8+2.7x9)=e2.7x⋅x8⋅(9+2.7x). \begin{aligned} f'(x) &= [g(x) \cdot h(x)]' = g'(x) \cdot h(x) + g(x) \cdot h'(x) \\ &= 9 x^{9 - 1} \cdot e^{2.7 x} + x^{9} \cdot e^{2.7 x} \cdot 2.7 \\ &= e^{2.7 x} \cdot(9 x^8 + 2.7 x^{9}) \\ &= e^{2.7 x} \cdot x^8 \cdot (9 + 2.7 x). \end{aligned} Evaluated at x=0.55x = 0.55, the answer is e2.7⋅0.55⋅0.558⋅(9+2.7⋅0.55)=0.387612. e^{2.7 \cdot 0.55} \cdot 0.55^8 \cdot (9 + 2.7 \cdot 0.55) = 0.387612. Thus, rounded to two digits we have f′(0.55)=0.39f'(0.55) = 0.39.


  2. Question

    A machine fills milk into 500ml packages. It is suspected that the machine is not working correctly and that the amount of milk filled differs from the setpoint μ0=500\mu_0 = 500. A sample of 243243 packages filled by the machine are collected. The sample mean y‾\bar{y} is equal to 509.6509.6 and the sample variance sn−12s^2_{n-1} is equal to 406.5406.5.

    Test the hypothesis that the amount filled corresponds on average to the setpoint. What is the absolute value of the t-test statistic?


    Solution

    The t-test statistic is calculated by: t=y‾−μ0sn−12n=509.6−500406.5243=7.422. \begin{aligned} t = \frac{\bar y - \mu_0}{\sqrt{\frac{s^2_{n-1}}{n}}} = \frac{509.6 - 500}{\sqrt{\frac{406.5}{243}}} = 7.422. \end{aligned} The absolute value of the t-test statistic is thus equal to 7.422.


  3. Question

    What is the distance between the two points p=(2,1)p = (2, 1) and q=(4,4)q = (4, 4) in a Cartesian coordinate system?


    Solution

    The distance dd of pp and qq is given by d2=(p1−q1)2+(p2−q2)2d^2 = (p_1 - q_1)^2 + (p_2 - q_2)^2 (Pythagorean formula).

    Hence d=(p1−q1)2+(p2−q2)2=(2−4)2+(1−4)2=3.606d = \sqrt{(p_1 - q_1)^2 + (p_2 - q_2)^2} = \sqrt{(2 - 4)^2 + (1 - 4)^2} = 3.606.

  6. Question

    A firm has the following production function: F(K,L)=KL2. F(K,L)= K L^{2}. The price for one unit of capital is pK=14p_K = 14 and the price for one unit of labor is pL=23p_L = 23. Minimize the costs of the firm considering its production function and given a target production output of 660 units.

    What is the amount of the input factor capital in this minimum?


    Solution

    Step 1: Formulating the minimization problem. minK,LC(K,L)=pKK+pLL=14K+23Lsubject to:F(K,L)=QKL2=660 \begin{aligned} \min_{K,L} C(K,L) & = p_K K + p_L L\\ & = 14 K + 23 L\\ \mbox{subject~to:} & F(K,L) = Q \\ & K L^{2} = 660 \end{aligned} Step 2: Lagrange function. ℒ(K,L,λ)=C(K,L)−λ(F(K,L)−Q)=14K+23L−λ(KL2−660) \begin{aligned} \mathcal{L}(K, L, \lambda) & = C(K, L) - \lambda (F(K, L) - Q) \\ & = 14 K + 23 L - \lambda (K L^{2} -660) \end{aligned} Step 3: First order conditions. ∂ℒ∂K=14−λL2=0∂ℒ∂L=23−2λKL2−1=0∂ℒ∂λ=−(KL2−660)=0 \begin{aligned} \frac{\partial {\mathcal {L}}}{\partial K} & = 14 - \lambda L^{2} = 0\\ \frac{\partial {\mathcal {L}}}{\partial L} & = 23 - {2} \lambda K L^{2 - 1} = 0 \\ \frac{\partial {\mathcal {L}}}{\partial \lambda} & = -(K L^{2}-660) = 0 \end{aligned} Step 4: Solve the system of equations for KK, LL, and λ\lambda.

    Solving the first two equations for λ\lambda and equating them gives: 14L2=232KL2−1K=232⋅14⋅L2−(2−1)K=2328⋅L \begin{aligned} \frac{14}{L^{2}} & = \frac{23}{{2} K L^{2 - 1}}\\ K & = \frac{23}{2 \cdot 14} \cdot L^{2 - (2 - 1)}\\ K & = \frac{23}{28} \cdot L \end{aligned} Substituting this in the optimization constraint gives: KL2=660(2328⋅L)L2=6602328L3=660L=(2823⋅660)13=9.2966121≈9.30K=2328⋅L=7.6365028≈7.64 \begin{aligned} K L^{2} & = 660\\ \left(\frac{23}{28}\cdot L \right) L^{2} & = 660\\ \frac{23}{28} L^{3} & = 660\\ L & = \left(\frac{28}{23} \cdot 660\right)^{\frac{1}{3}} = 9.2966121 \approx 9.30\\ K & = \frac{23}{28} \cdot L = 7.6365028 \approx 7.64 \end{aligned} Given the target output, the optimal amount of the input factor capital is K=7.64K = 7.64.
