  1. Question

    Which of the following statements about Switzerland is correct?


    1. The Swiss national holiday is August 1.
    2. Italian is an official language in Switzerland.
    3. Switzerland is part of the European Union (EU).
    4. The currency in Switzerland is the Euro.
    5. Zurich is the capital of Switzerland.

    Solution

    1. True. The establishment of the Swiss Confederation is traditionally dated to August 1, 1291.
    2. True. The official languages are: German, French, Italian, Romansh.
    3. False. Switzerland is part of the Schengen Area but not the EU.
    4. False. The currency is the Swiss Franc (CHF).
    5. False. There is no de jure capital but the de facto capital of Switzerland is Bern.

  2. Question

    Under the assumptions of the Gauss-Markov theorem the errors of a linear regression model need to be:


    1. identically distributed
    2. homoscedastic
    3. independent
    4. uncorrelated
    5. zero

    Solution

    Under the assumptions of the Gauss-Markov theorem the errors of a linear regression model need to be uncorrelated, homoscedastic, and with mean zero.


    1. False. No distribution assumption is needed.
    2. True. The errors need to be homoscedastic with finite variance.
    3. False. Independence is not assumed, only lack of correlation.
    4. True. The errors need to be uncorrelated.
    5. False. Only their conditional expectation needs to be zero.

  3. Question

    A survey with 46 persons was conducted to analyze the design of an advertising campaign. Each respondent was asked to evaluate the overall impression of the advertisement on an eleven-point scale from 0 (bad) to 10 (good). The evaluations are summarized separately with respect to type of occupation of the respondents in the following figure.

    To analyze the influence of occupation on the evaluation of the advertisement an analysis of variance was performed:

      Res.Df    RSS Df Sum of Sq     F   Pr(>F)
1     45 35.660                            
2     42 28.429  3     7.231 3.561 0.022015

    Which of the following statements are correct?


    1. A one-sided alternative was tested for the mean values.
    2. The test statistic is larger than 13.413.4.
    3. It can be shown that the evaluation of the respondents depends on their occupation. (Significance level 55%)
    4. The fraction of explained variance is larger than 2323%.
    5. The fraction of explained variance is smaller than 3838%.

    Solution

    In order to be able to answer the questions the fraction of explained variance has to be determined. The residual sum of squares when using only a single overall mean value (𝑅𝑆𝑆0\mathit{RSS}_0) as well as the residual sum of squares when allowing different mean values given occupation (𝑅𝑆𝑆1\mathit{RSS}_1) are required. Both are given in the RSS column of the ANOVA table. The fraction of explained variance is given by 1−𝑅𝑆𝑆1/𝑅𝑆𝑆0=1−28.429/35.66=0.2031 - \mathit{RSS}_1/\mathit{RSS}_0 = 1 - 28.429/35.66 = 0.203.

    The statements above can now be evaluated as right or wrong.


    1. False. An ANOVA always tests the null hypothesis, that all mean values are equal against the alternative hypothesis that they are different.
    2. False. The test statistic is F=3.561F = 3.561 and hence not larger than 13.413.4.
    3. True. The pp value is 0.0220.022 and hence significant. It can be shown that the evaluations differ with respect to the occupation of the respondents.
    4. False. The fraction of explained variance is 0.2030.203 and hence not larger than 0.23.
    5. True. The fraction of explained variance is 0.2030.203 and hence smaller than 0.38.

  4. Question

    In the following figure the distributions of a variable given by two samples (A and B) are represented by parallel boxplots. Which of the following statements are correct? (Comment: The statements are either about correct or clearly wrong.)


    1. The location of both distributions is about the same.
    2. Both distributions contain no outliers.
    3. The spread in sample A is clearly bigger than in B.
    4. The skewness of both samples is similar.
    5. Distribution A is about symmetric.

    Solution

    1. True. Both distributions have a similar location.
    2. True. Both distributions have no observations which deviate more than 1.5 times the interquartile range from the box.
    3. False. The interquartile range in sample A is not clearly bigger than in B.
    4. True. The skewness of both distributions is similar, both are right-skewed.
    5. False. Distribution A is right-skewed.
