  1. Question

    What is the derivative of f(x)=x2e2.8xf(x) = x^{2} e^{2.8 x}, evaluated at x=0.63x = 0.63?


    Solution

    Using the product rule for f(x)=g(x)⋅h(x)f(x) = g(x) \cdot h(x), where g(x):=x2g(x) := x^{2} and h(x):=e2.8xh(x) := e^{2.8 x}, we obtain f′(x)=[g(x)⋅h(x)]′=g′(x)⋅h(x)+g(x)⋅h′(x)=2x2−1⋅e2.8x+x2⋅e2.8x⋅2.8=e2.8x⋅(2x1+2.8x2)=e2.8x⋅x1⋅(2+2.8x). \begin{aligned} f'(x) &= [g(x) \cdot h(x)]' = g'(x) \cdot h(x) + g(x) \cdot h'(x) \\ &= 2 x^{2 - 1} \cdot e^{2.8 x} + x^{2} \cdot e^{2.8 x} \cdot 2.8 \\ &= e^{2.8 x} \cdot(2 x^1 + 2.8 x^{2}) \\ &= e^{2.8 x} \cdot x^1 \cdot (2 + 2.8 x). \end{aligned} Evaluated at x=0.63x = 0.63, the answer is e2.8⋅0.63⋅0.631⋅(2+2.8⋅0.63)=13.838392. e^{2.8 \cdot 0.63} \cdot 0.63^1 \cdot (2 + 2.8 \cdot 0.63) = 13.838392. Thus, rounded to two digits we have f′(0.63)=13.84f'(0.63) = 13.84.
